Delicacies

Palawan is well-known for its natural beauty, but this Philippine island paradise also ups its game when it comes to local cuisine.
If you are planning a trip to this island, you should consider sampling these wonderful delights.

BAKER'S HILL HOPIA

Baker's Hill in Puerto Princesa is without a doubt the province's most famous bakery. It makes a variety of pastries and baked delicacies for travelers to sample and take home. The excellent Baker's Hill hopia is one of the goods you should try. These delectable sweet buns are available in a variety of flavors, including monggo, ube, and pork.


Crocodile Sisig at Kinabuch’s Bar & Grill

The renowned Crocodile Sisig is something to satisfy your taste senses when visiting Palawan. It is one of the unusual foods on the island that tourists like trying. Crocodile flesh is cooked with other delightful ingredients and spices before being served on a sizzling platter, similar to the traditional sisig meal. For a more savory experience, the dish also includes an egg, bell peppers, pieces of onion, and other seasonings.


Honey Nougat at La Terrasse Restaurant Café

Palawan's Honey Nougat dessert at La Terrassee Restaurant Café takes our taste senses to the sweet side. Dessert consisting mostly of Palawan nougat, wild honey, and whole cashew nuts. It is the ultimate sweet delicacy that you should enjoy on your Palawan trip. This is a traditional local dish produced from the island's natural ingredients.


Lechon Kawali at Blue Palawan

The crisp and delicate meat of lechon kawali is everything. While Palawan is well-known for its seafood platters, the island's lechon kawali is also a must-try. This pig belly dish is best served as a viand (a particularly excellent and savory meal) or as a pulutan (simply referred to a bar food). This local delicacy is delicious in whichever way you choose to enjoy it.


Super Creamy Halo Halo at Noki Nocs Savory House

The creamy halo-halo is another Palawan sweet delight not to be missed. This refreshing delicacy is made up of a variety of ingredients (thus the name, which translates to "assorted mix"): crushed ice, a variety of sweet beans, milk, corn, and a topping of ice cream and leche flan. It's the ideal drink to keep you cool in the midday heat.


Tamilok at Kinabuch’s Grill & Bar

The famed tamilok is another unusual dish that we dare you to sample on your Palawan adventure. Tamilok is a type of ceviche made from woodworm mollusks. For a more savory taste, it is often served with vinegar and chile. It may not be the most visually appealing meal, but many vouch to its deliciousness.
