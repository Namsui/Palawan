History

Life is about having the courage to venture into the unknown, but a little knowledge is also helpful.
Here's a quick history of the Palawan Province.

Name Origin


It is thought that the name came from the Chinese phrase Pa-Lao-Yu, which means “the land of beautiful harbors." Others believe the term derives from the Indian word "Palawans," which means "territory." While others believe it is derived from the Spanish word "Paragua," as Palawan resembles a closed umbrella.

Place History


Palawan was one of the first locations the Chinese arrived in the Philippines, crossing land bridges directly into the area. They first named the area. Since then, the spelling and pronunciation have changed a bit (from Pa-Lao-Yu to what is now Palawan). Palawan became the center of Chinese trade.

Malay people began coming into the area in the 12th century, utilizing it as an agricultural foundation for developing a new settlement. They lived reasonably peacefully in the area until the early 17th century, when the Spanish began to take control. They constructed missions in Palawan and began converting the locals. Although the area's diversified culture allows for the practice of many other religions, Roman Catholicism is the area's largest religion today.

The Spanish lingered in the area until the 20th century. There was a revolution at the time, and the American government assumed control of the area, forming a local administration and supporting local growth. Puerto Princesa was designated as the region's capital at that time. The region was destroyed during WWII and again at the turn of the twenty-first century when terrorists abducted visitors to the area. Despite this, the neighborhood is thriving, and visitors are urged to visit.
