Tourist Spots

This list of Palawan tourist attractions will ensure that you have the greatest possible experience during your visit to Palawan. The Philippines, with its blend of leisure, excitement, sun, and beach, is a trip that everyone should put on their bucket list!

Puerto Princesa Underground River

(Puerto Princesa)

Paddling through the cave system will appeal to nature enthusiasts. When you take a Puerto Princesa Underground River trip, you can expect to encounter stalactites and stalagmites of all forms and sizes.

The trip will take you through a section of the 8.2-kilometer subterranean river that runs underneath the protected cave, giving you plenty of opportunity to marvel at the rock formations you'll see along the way. Within the cave, you'll see stalactites that look like vegetables, dinosaurs, and religious images.

Tubbataha Reef

(Cagayancillo Islands)

The Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park, located in the southern section of the island, is one of the top diving destinations in the Philippines and the globe. This magnificent place can be found in the seas of Cagayancillo town and is also designated as one of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the Philippines.

In fact, over a thousand marine creatures, including sea turtles, sharks, manta rays, and clownfish, may be found here. Aside from the aquatic critters, around 100 bird species may be seen here, with some of them even building nests in the trees that cover the neighboring islands.

Kayangan Lake

(Coron)

Coron is a relaxed place with a lot to offer visitors. Lakes, lagoons, and magnificent white-sand beaches may be found on the island. This tropical getaway allows you to unwind, relax, and get away from the everyday grind. Kayangan Lake is one of the main sights on Coron island-hopping trips.

When you approach the cove, you'll have to walk a flight of stairs to a vantage point to see the most famous sight of this site. It's the most well-known attraction in the neighborhood, and it's a great site to snap photographs before heading to the lake. It's the high point of Coron's ultimate island-hopping excursion.

Twin Lagoon

(Coron)

The Twin Lagoon in Coron Island, Palawan province, consists of two lovely lagoons separated by tall limestone cliffs. Visiting the lagoons is an exciting trip.

They are only accessible by boat from the town main, which will take you through a maze of massive karst walls. You may arrange a Twin Lagoon trip if you want a hassle-free adventure.

Boats dock on the first lagoon, and tourists must swim from there to a decrepit wooden ladder leading to the second. Instead, during low tide, you may wade into a tunnel that connects both lagoons.

Seven Commandos Beach

(El Nido)

Going to Seven Commandos Beach is like entering a another world. It boasts a lengthy stretch of beautiful white sand and a swinging line of coconut palms beneath its stunning limestone cliffs. The beach is included in El Nido island hopping cruises, which also include the popular Big and Small Lagoons.

The journey normally begins with a stop to Seven Commandos, where guests may swim, snorkel, and eat delicious coconut juice. Boats will dock for approximately 45 minutes. After that, you may relax and rest in the region before seeing the other tourist attractions, particularly those included in the island-hopping trip in El Nido. It's the ideal way to spend a day in El Nido.

Mt. Tapyas

(Coron)

The trip to Mt. Tapyas' viewing deck is worthwhile. Despite the 724-step journey, it'll be worth it since you'll get to enjoy Coron's unique perspective, which improves as you get higher.

Mt. Tapyas is an excellent half-day excursion for people searching for an alternative to the traditional Coron island-hopping trips.

Feel the fresh air and see the city from a panoramic point of view as you slowly reach the top. Rest a while before going down; wait until sunset to watch the blue sky turn red and then dark.

Snake Island

(El Nido)

This island, also known as Vigan Island, earned its unusual name from a 3-meter-long S-shaped sandbar.

Snake Island trips, like any other island in El Nido, are popular because to its crystal clear blue seas. It is one of the islands that will provide you with the sensation of wandering in the midst of the sea.

Snorkeling near the shoreline allows you to see marine life such as little fish, sea turtles, corals, and starfish. Snake Island is also a tropical paradise with lush vegetation and a plethora of mangroves, where monkeys are frequently spotted.

Calauit Safari Park

(Busuanga)

Calauit Safari Park is the Philippines' sole game reserve and wildlife sanctuary, home to hundreds of endangered and unique species. Its permanent species include African animals transferred from Kenya in 1976, such as the reticulated giraffe and the Grevy's zebra, the world's most endangered zebra species.

These creatures, as well as mouse deer, bearcats, and the Calamian deer, may be found freely traversing the sanctuary's 3,700-hectare area. Visitors on a Calauit Safari Park trip are permitted to undertake supervised feeding to giraffes while on foot, delivering an exciting experience comparable to Kenyan safaris.

Fort Santa Isabel

(Taytay)

Fort Santa Isabel, also known as the Taytay Fort, is a fortification that goes back to 1667, when it was initially constructed of wood. It was named after Queen Isabella II of Spain. It was eventually reconstructed with coral stone and finished in 1738.

During the Spanish period in the Philippines, this coastal stronghold was primarily used to protect Taytay against Muslim warrior-raiders. Whenever Muslim soldiers approached the little town, the Spanish army would fire guns from the stronghold.

Siete Pecados

(Coron)

In a Coron day trip, Siete Pecados is one of the most frequently suggested tourist destinations. What's great about this marine park is that all seven of its islands are alive with marine life and varied marine species.

The region is famed for its colorful marine life, which is enthralling when you plunge into its blue seas. The diving location Siete Pecados is the closest to Coron city. The boat brings you straight into the midst of the islets in only a few minutes.
