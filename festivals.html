Festivities

Festivals in Palawan are more than just occasions for joy. They help Palawenos stay connected to their heritage and traditions. Festivals provide opportunity to maintain the origins of Palawan's
population despite changing times.

Arawedan Festival

January 23rd-24th

The Arawedan Festival is a celebration to be held in Port Barton, San Vicente Municipality, from January 23 to January 24. During this festival, the municipality will introduce you to various attractions of the municipality, such as beaches, marine protected areas and parks. The event will also feature a program that highlights the cultural elements of Palawan.

Puerto Princesa City Foundation Day and Balayong Festival

March 14th

The 14th of March marks the founding anniversary of Puerto Princesa City, the "City in the Forest." This establishment anniversary coincides with the Balayong Festival, which promotes the Palawan Cherry, which is quite similar to the Cherry Blossoms of Japan. This tree's flowers only bloom in February and March, which is roughly during the spring season in non-tropical regions.

Kulambo Festival

March 15th-18th

El Nido, one of the most renowned and distinctive islands in the province of Palawan, is hosting this event. The eye-catching costumes of the street parade participants, who used kulambo, also known as mosquito nets, in their outfit, are the main attraction of this occasion.

Kaniyogan Festival

March 16th-22nd

Every March 16-22, Brooke's Point hosts the Kaniyog'n Festival, which incorporates coconut goods, coconut-themed floats, and street dance.

"Kaniyogan," or coconut plantation, abounds in this hamlet and has become a significant source of income for its residents. The festival's goal is to promote the many products obtained from the "tree of life." Trade fairs, cultural and modern events, sporting activities, and a beauty contest are all part of the festivities.

Rizal Town Founding Anniversary & Tau't Bato Festival

April 17th-21st

Araw ng Katutubo (Day of the Ancient), Tau't Bato Festival, in which the government conducts tribal sports, tribal song contests, and dance competitions, are also commemorated.

The commemoration and festival will take place from April 17 to April 21. These events are aimed at indigenous people in order to assist and share their culture with other Filipinos and visitors.

Kalugtan Festival

April 28th- May 1st

From April 28 to May 1, the gorgeous village of El Nido, Palawan, celebrates nature and culture with the 6th Kalugtan Arts Festival. The celebration, which begins on Earth Day (April 22), will emphasize the importance of environmental preservation while also highlighting the region's rich creative tradition.

The word "kalugtan" comes from the Cuyuno word "lugta," which means "earth." The festival, organized by the Kalugtan Arts Guild with the cooperation of El Nido Mayor Leonor Corral, includes seminars and workshops on several art disciplines, as well as exhibitions and a variety of special activities.

Karagatan Festival

1st week of May

During the month of the ocean, the city celebrates the Karagatan Festival in the first week of May. It was initially introduced on May 1, 1998. The event highlights man's relationship with the water.

This is one of the several Puerto Princesa festivities hosted on the city's west coast's beautiful beaches. This four-day festival includes activities such as coastal cleanup, beach volleyball and football, sand sculpting, boat sailing, kite flying, banca racing, mountain bike race and trekking, sea trekking, football, tug-of-war, and film screening. And all of this culminates in a large beach party and concert, as well as a Westkini bikini competition.

Pasinggatan Festival

May 1st-4th

Singing and dancing are two of the most popular pleasures in the Philippines, as seen by the Pasinggatan Festival, which takes place from May 1-4 in Taytay, Palawan.

It is derived from the term "singgat," which means "to shine or gleam," and is a suitable name for a festival whose festivities bring out the best in individuals, from their abilities to their personalities.

Manunggul Festival

May 15th

Manunggul Festival is an annual event held on May 15th in the municipality of Quezon, Palawan. The Manunggul Festival is a salute to your uniqueness, an affirmation of the togetherness that enables you to thrive, and a celebration of Quezon's importance in Philippine history and ethnography.

May this occasion unite you in activities that will perpetuate the harmony that exists among your citizens, as well as encourage you to guard the unique natural and historical legacy that you have the privilege of holding.

Baragatan sa Palawan

3rd week of June

The celebration of Baragatan sa Palawan, which takes place every third week of June, is perhaps one of the most important festivals for the people of Palawan.

The festival focuses on the province's historical, cultural, agricultural, and trade potentials, with involvement from municipalities, the commercial sector, and non-governmental organizations. "Baragatan" is a gathering, convergence, and assemblage of people and culture, derived from the Cuyunon (a native language) term "bagat" which meaning "to meet."

Pista Y ang Kagueban-

3rd week of June

Pista Y ang Kagueban, or Feast of the Forest, is one of the municipal governments of Puerto Princesa's methods of maintaining environment and replenishing its woods.

This activity helps to preserve the environment, which is a priority for the administration of Puerto Princesa, which organizes the tree planting every third week of June.

Malagnang Festival

June 19th-21st

The Malagnang Festival is a yearly festival held on the picturesque beach of San Vicente from June 19 to 21, during which local people and visitors participate in a variety of events, competitions, and cultural presentations.

The term "Malagnang" literally means "Mud" or "Muddy" in the local dialect, and it refers to the fertile soil that provided life and wealth to the town's early residents. The Malagnang festival is regarded to be a strange celebration among other holidays in the Philippines since it praises an earthy item called mud rather than a particular local dish or culture.

Pista ng Dalampasigan

June 24th

Every June 24th, Sofronio, Espanola celebrates Pista ng Dalampasigan. This operation is credited with providing coastal cleaning as well as mangrove reforestation.

Kasadyaan Festival

August 28th

Kasadyaan is derived from the Cuyunon word "sadya," which means "merrymaking."
Every August 28th, Coron, Palawan celebrates a gratitude feast for all favors received in honor of its patron saint, San Agustin. It is manifested via cultural performances, sporting events, and other activities.

Cuyo Fiesta

August

During the month of August, the municipality of Cuyo in Palawan celebrates their fiesta. Their festival is identical to the Ati-Atihan Festival. Participants in this event blacken their skin and generally dance with abandon to the rhythmic beat of the drums.

Guests will be able to feast their eyes on colorful parades, dancing performances, musical presentations, and a re-enactment of San Agustin's battle with the indigenous during the Cuyo Fiesta.

Anihan/Palay Festival

October 25th-31st

The Anihan (Harvest) Festival is held in Narra, Palawan, every October 25-31.

It is a celebration of thankfulness for the abundant harvest that the people got during the year, as well as a prayer that the crop in the coming year will be just as plentiful. The Anihan Festival, currently known as the Palay Festival, is one of Narra's most well-known events, showcasing the municipality's goods.

Light a Tree Festival

1st of December

This is one of the most anticipated occasions for every Puerto Princesan on the first of December.

Years ago, the lighting of the huge tree took place in Mendoza Park, but it was eventually relocated to the Puerto Princesa Baywalk to accommodate more people. While waiting for the tree to be lit, visitors are entertained by choirs from several schools, which include elementary, high school, and college and university students.

Paskuhan at Kantahan sa Kapitolyo

between the 15th and the 23rd of December

One of Palawan's most popular celebrations takes place between the 15th and the 23rd of December. Many locals and frequent tourists refer to this as the Paskuhan at Kantahan sa Kapitolyo.

This yearly ceremony is held in the Provincial Capital in Puerto Princesa City to convey the Christmas spirit. Carol Singing, Parada ng Lechon, a parade of a cooked pig, which is a local delicacy in the land, and Tunog Tao, which is an interesting display of musical prowess and rhythm by the participants by touching or gently slapping a part of the body to produce a sound, are all activities during this particular festivity.
